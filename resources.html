About This Guide

Sensei Doug Walsh has assembled these resources to support students in their study and practice of Karate-Do. Use the hyperlinks throughout this page to access instructional videos, reference documents, and articles that will deepen your understanding of Shotokan technique, tradition, and philosophy.

The foundation of karate is kihon (basic techniques), kata (forms), and kumite (sparring). Upon these three rest all technique, all speed, all strength, and all progress in karate.

Philosophy

Dojo Kun

The five principles of Karate-Do — the rules of the dojo and the rules of the Karate-ka outside of the dojo. Recited at the close of every class.

Shotokan Dojo Kun

Read right to left in the Japanese tradition

  • 01
    Seek perfection of characterHitotsu — Jinkaku kansei ni tsutomeru koto
  • 02
    Be faithfulHitotsu — Makoto no michi o mamoru koto
  • 03
    EndeavourHitotsu — Doryoku no seishin o yashinau koto
  • 04
    Respect othersHitotsu — Reigi o omonzuru koto
  • 05
    Refrain from violent behaviourHitotsu — Kekki no yuu o imashimuru koto

Advancement

Rank Testing

There are 10 Kyu and 10 Dan ranks in JKA karate. All ranks are officially certified by the Japan Karate Association.

Kyu/Dan Testing Syllabus

To obtain certification for each rank, you must fulfill the certification requirements and complete the testing procedure. Testing — or grading — occurs about every three months, must be pre-approved by your Sensei, and is carried out by a panel of instructors authorized as JKA technical examiners.

All certifications are issued by and recognized by the Japan Karate Association worldwide. Students may also test under Master Mikami directly and attend his special clinics throughout the year.
